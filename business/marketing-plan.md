
# marketing-plan.md

## Positioning
The ultimate safety net for developers, ensuring uninterrupted code deployment when hosting platforms vanish or are deprecated.

### Alternatives
1. Your code's last resort: automatic migration and deployment when your hosting provider disappears.
2. The developer's insurance policy: seamless transition to new infrastructure if your current platform is sunset.
3. Vendor lock-in prevention tool: automated, secure migration to protect your code and users.

## ICP (Ideal Customer Personas)
1. **Alex, Senior Full-Stack Engineer**  
   - Daily job: Manages 5+ servers, deploys to Heroku/Netlify, monitors uptime.  
   - Budget: $5,000/year (SaaS subscription).  
2. **Maya, DevOps Lead**  
   - Daily job: Oversees 20+ servers, uses AWS/Azure, mitigates vendor risk.  
   - Budget: $15,000/year (enterprise plan).  
3. **Carlos, Solo SaaS Developer**  
   - Daily job: Hosts on Vercel, builds 1-2 products, fears sudden deprecation.  
   - Budget: $2,000/year (basic plan).

## Channels (Top 3 with CAC Estimate)
1. **Technical Conferences (DevOpsDays, CloudNativeCon)**  
   - Target: DevOps/Engineers, hands-on demos.  
   - CAC: $500 (high-touch, high conversion).  
2. **Developer Communities (Stack Overflow, r/devops, GitHub Discussions)**  
   - Target: Problem-driven content, use case examples.  
   - CAC: $200 (organic, content-led).  
3. **Email Marketing to Tech Publications (InfoQ, DZone)**  
   - Target: Decision-makers, industry insights.  
   - CAC: $300 (niche, high intent).
